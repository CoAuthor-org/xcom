## Section 3: Building in Public 2026 – Strategic Transparency (Not Full Disclosure)
The game changed. LLM cloning is real. Share your full tech stack or prompt library and someone clones you in a weekend. Audience ≠ customers. The new moat is customer intelligence and execution speed.
What to Share vs Hide (Golden Rules):

Share: Wins, struggles, customer “aha” moments, learnings, patterns, polls, prototypes. Bite-sized weekly progress. Make audience feel part of the journey.
Hide: Exact architecture, prompt engineering, pricing experiments, distribution channels that actually work, proprietary tech.
Best Practices: Define goals first. Stay consistent. Engage replies. Use threads + video updates. Radical transparency on process, strategic opacity on blueprints.

Proven Playbook from Top Founders:

Post daily/weekly small wins → accountability + feedback.
Turn customer interviews into public insights → authority without giving away the farm.
“Build in public” still works if your moat is speed. Clones can’t copy 6 months of real user understanding.
